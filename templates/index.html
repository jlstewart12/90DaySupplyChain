2020 Grain Inspections

These charts display the volumes of U.S. grains inspected for export from major U.S. port regions and countries of destination.

The selected dates range from from 1/2/2020 - 3/26/2020

When clicking a specific grain, you will be able to view each destination where grains were exported to.

Choose to view between Pounds and Metric Tons.

Choose date:

Key Takeaways:

  • Mexico, Japan, and China are the top 3 destinations for grain exports.
  • Soybeans and corn were the only grains to show a consistent trend throughout the time period. Soybeans decreased in volume from January-March by close to 6.5 billion lbs. Corn increased during this time by just under 6 billion lbs .
  • Highest volume for soybeans was on 1/30/2020.
  • Highest volume for wheat was on 2/27/2020.
  • Highest volume for corn was on 3/26/2020.
  • Highest volume for sorghum was on 1/23/2020.
  • Sorghum has the lowest volume of grains transported overall.
  • Soybeans had the largest decrease in volume.
  • Corn had the largest increase in volume.
